Feature Selection in Economic Predictive Analytics

The objective of this project has been to explore feature selection techniques for economic predictive analytics. Feature selection is the process of choosing the best subset from the available features. The features are data attributes used to build predictive models.

Feature Selection Method Summary
Domain Based Criteria
Which is more predictive: Time-Series from a balanced set of series (Money, Banking, & Finance, Population, Employment, & Labor Markets, National Accounts, Production & Business Activity, Prices, International Data) versus selecting time-series all from one category.
Normalized data versus raw data: which offers more accuracy?
The normalization methods considered are:
Min-Max normalization
Decimal scaling
Aggregation
Predicting over an aggregated data set is more economical that over the pre-aggregated set. Is the relative accuracy between aggregated and raw feature sets the same?
Data Transformations
Does a log transformation improve the accuracy of a predictor?
Information Theoretic measures
Granger Causality

Questions of interest include: What are some methods for selecting features and what are their advantages and disadvantages; which methods are easiest to use; what is the range of effectiveness between methods; how do methods respond to dimension size?
